FireSR: Super-Resolution and Segmentation of Burned Areas

Eric Brune*, Yifang Ban
KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Division of Geoinformatics
NeurIPS 2024
Paper Supplementary Code Dataset

Wildfire events in Canada ranging from 2017 to 2023 with a burned area larger than 2000 HA.

Abstract

The increasing frequency and severity of wildfires due to climate change necessitate high-resolution daily monitoring. While MODIS provides daily global coverage at a coarse resolution of 500 meters in its short-wave infrared band, Sentinel-2 offers higher resolution at 20 meters but with a five-day revisit time. Addressing the gap between the need for high spatial and temporal resolution monitoring and the limitations of existing satellite data, we introduce FireSR, a dataset that includes all wildfire events in Canada from 2017 to 2023 larger than 2000 hectares. The dataset comprises pre- and post-fire Sentinel-2 images, post-fire MODIS images, and National Burned Area Composite (NBAC) polygons. We demonstrate the capability of FireSR through three models: a Conditional Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Model (DDPM) for super-resolution (SR) of Sentinel-2 pre-fire and MODIS post-fire images, a U-Net for segmentation, and a multitask DDPM for simultaneous super-resolution and segmentation. Our experiments show that learning both tasks enhances performance in each, thus enabling daily high-resolution wildfire monitoring. The main objective of FireSR is to stimulate further research and innovation in achieving daily high-resolution wildfire monitoring, thereby supporting effective wildfire response and management.
