Abstract

FireSR: Super-Resolution and Segmentation of Burned Areas

The increasing frequency and severity of wildfires due to climate change necessitate high-resolution daily monitoring. While MODIS provides daily global coverage at a coarse resolution of 500 meters in its short-wave infrared band, Sentinel-2 offers higher resolution at 20 meters but with a five-day revisit time, limiting its effectiveness for continuous monitoring. To address this challenge, we introduce FireSR, a dataset comprising all wildfire events in Canada from 2017 to 2022 larger than 2000 hectares. The dataset includes pre- and post-fire Sentinel-2 images, post-fire MODIS images, and National Burned Area Composite (NBAC) polygons. We demonstrate the effectiveness of FireSR through three models: a Conditional Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Model (DDPM) for super-resolution, a U-Net for segmentation, and a multitask DDPM for simultaneous super-resolution and segmentation. Our experiments show that combining these tasks enhances performance, enabling daily high-resolution wildfire monitoring. FireSR aims to advance machine learning techniques for remote sensing and provides a valuable resource for effective wildfire management and response. The dataset is openly accessible at this link.
